Open Source

Scala.js

Core Contributor

Sep 2013 - Present

  • Scala.js (http://www.scala-js.org/) is a compiler that converts Scala code into equivalent, executable Javascript. It allows you to write websites in an expressive, type-safe language and share code between client and server. Scala.js has thousands of people using it for real work, and a healthy ecosystem of open-source libraries.

  • I am not the author of the original Scala.js project (that's Sebastien Doraene), but I can confidently say it would not exist as it does today without my help.

Early Adopter

  • Pioneered usage of Scala.js, demonstrating its effectiveness in writing real-world projects through https://lihaoyi.github.io/scala-js-games and https://lihaoyi.github.io/roll
  • Pushed for several usability improvements that helped make Scala.js actually usable (edit-refresh speed, debuggability, etc.)
  • Contributed a dozen commits and many more bug-reports in the process.

Community

  • Built the community from nothing, to thousands of active users. I presented at dozens of conferences and meetups to show off Scala.js and attract interest in the project.
  • Highlights include Live Coding Scala.js, Scala.js' debut talk at the SF Scala Meetup, which has been watched over 5,000 times. My Scala.js-related videos have had >20,000 views on all platforms. Full listing below.
  • Wrote the free, interactive e-book Hands-on Scala.js (http://www.hands-on-scala-js.com)
  • Scala by the Bay 2015 had five talks about Scala.js, none of them by me. The community has a life of its own!

Ecosystem

  • Initially built the Scala.js library ecosystem from nothing. A compiler or language without libraries is useless, and I built out enough of them that people coming to Scala.js could use it to do real work.
  • In total my libraries are getting >40,000 downloads a month from the package repository, and every Scala.js project in existence uses some of them. See https://github.com/lihaoyi to find out more.
